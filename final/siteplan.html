Brazzaville Tech Learners Hub

A community space for students and professionals learning software development and AI — meetups, tutorials, and practical API integrations.

Site name

"brazza-techub.org"

This name describes that the site is about a technolog field and it's located in Brazzaville

Scenarios

1. Prospective learner: A student or early-career developer looks for hands-on workshops, tutorials, and community support. They should be able to quickly find upcoming events, learning resources, and registration options to join.

2. Local employer or recruiter: A company hiring engineers checks the hub to find well-prepared candidates, sponsor meetups, or post internship & job opportunities. They should find contact information, volunteer opportunities, and ways to support the community.

3. Community mentor or organiser: An experienced developer or instructor wants to host a session, share learning materials, or collaborate on curriculum. The hub should make it easy to submit events, share resources, and connect with learners.

Site purpose

The Brazzaville Tech Learners Hub connects students, professionals, and industry partners across Brazzaville with local learning opportunities, community events, and mentorship. The site provides an accessible, centralized point to register for meetups and workshops, explore learning materials, find volunteer and sponsorship opportunities, and discover ways to get involved in building a stronger local tech ecosystem.

  • Promote events, workshops, and learning resources.
  • Connect learners with mentors and employers.
  • Encourage community-driven projects and knowledge sharing.
  • Share practical information for joining, sponsoring, or partnering with the hub.

Color Schema

The site uses CSS custom properties defined in hub.css for consistent theming. The variables and their intended usage are listed below:

  • --primary – Indigo Blue: #0B5FFF (Primary brand color — headings)
  • --accent – Blue: #0066CC (Header and subtle accents; used for the navigation background)
  • --accent-teal – Teal: #00BFA6 (Interactive elements such as link highlights, secondary CTAs, and success messages)
  • --neutral-900 – Charcoal: #0F1724 (Body copy and footer background)
  • --bg – White: #FFFFFF (Main background color)
  • --card – Card background: #F7F9FC (Cards and section backgrounds)

Design notes:

  • Prefer --primary for prominent CTAs and brand identity; use --accent for navigation and supplementary accents.
  • Reserve --accent-teal for link states, status messages and helpful micro-interactions.
  • Use --neutral-900 for body copy to keep good contrast against --bg.
  • All color choices should follow WCAG contrast guidelines; use the variables to enforce consistent accessibility checks.

Typography

Typography should be modern and legible for longer reads and learning materials. Recommended choices:

  • Headings: 'Poppins', 'Montserrat', or bold 'Inter' (Google Font) — used for section titles and CTAs to be bold and clear.
  • Body: 'Inter', 'Roboto', or system sans-serif stack — used for paragraphs, lists, and form text for readability at small sizes.
  • Fallback stack: system-ui, -apple-system, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif

Suggested defaults: base font size 16px, line-height 1.5, heading scale with responsive sizes. Use bold weights for emphasized items and ensure adequate contrast between text and background colors.
